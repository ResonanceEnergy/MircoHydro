> "The propelling fluid acts upon the surfaces of the [discs] through viscosity and adhesion, delivering nearly all of its momentum efficiently." - Tesla

**Design:**
- Multiple closely-spaced discs (0.4-1.6mm gap)
- Fluid flows spirally inward between discs
- Viscous shear transfers momentum (not impact like conventional turbines!)
- Claims: 95%+ efficiency potential!

**Why It Could Work:**
1. **No Blade Losses:** Viscous shear has no shock, no separation, no cavitation
2. **Self-Adjusting:** Works across wide flow range (Reynolds adaptation)
3. **Simple Manufacturing:** Just flat discs (vs complex 3D blades)

**Why It Might NOT Work:**
1. **Unproven at Scale:** Tesla's prototypes were <1 kW; scaling to 5 kW unproven
2. **Disc Spacing Critical:** 0.4mm tolerance over 1m diameter = challenging manufacturing
3. **Low Torque:** Viscous forces weak → requires very high RPM (10,000+) → generator challenges
4. **Disc Flexing:** Water pressure can bow discs → rub/crash

**R&D Plan:**
1. **Phase A (6 months, $15k):** Build 500W prototype, test efficiency vs crossflow
2. **Phase B (12 months, $35k):** If >80% proven, build 5kW full-scale prototype
3. **Phase C (24 months, $100k):** Field test, optimize, validate long-term reliability

**Potential Efficiency:** IF proven: 73% → 88% (+15 pp turbine efficiency!)  
**Risk:** 60% chance of failure (unproven technology)  
**Expected Value:** 0.4 × 88% + 0.6 × 73% = **79% (weighted average)**

**DECISION:** Pursue Tesla turbine as Phase 3 R&D project. DO NOT include in product roadmap until validated.

### **3.2 MAGNETOHYDRODYNAMIC (MHD) GENERATOR**
**Visionary Sources:** Hannes Alfvén (Nobel Prize 1970), Safire Project (plasma physics)

**Breakthrough Concept:**
- Moving conductive fluid (seeded water) through magnetic field → direct electricity generation
- NO mechanical moving parts! (no turbine, no generator, no bearings!)
- Potential 90%+ efficiency (no mechanical losses!)

**How MHD Works:**
```
B (magnetic field) ⊥ v (water velocity) → E (electric field) induced
Conductive water (+ salt or ionization) carries current
Electrodes collect current → DC electricity directly!
```

**Why It Could Work:**
1. **Direct Conversion:** Kinetic → Electric (no mechanical intermediate)
2. **No Moving Parts:** Zero wear, infinite lifespan
3. **Scalable:** Works from mW to GW scale
4. **High Conductivity Path:** Seawater or salt-seeded water works

**Why It Might NOT Work:**
1. **Requires STRONG Magnets:** 1-2 Tesla field = $10k+ in rare-earth magnets
2. **Requires HIGH Velocity:** MHD power ∝ v³ → needs >10 m/s (high head or nozzle)
3. **Electrode Erosion:** Electrolysis at electrodes → corrosion, maintenance
4. **Low Power Density:** Needs large duct volume for kW-scale power

**R&D Plan:**
1. **Phase A (12 months, $25k):** Build 100W MHD proof-of-concept (seeded water, 1T magnet)
2. **Phase B (24 months, $75k):** If >50% efficiency, scale to 1 kW prototype
3. **Phase C (36 months, $200k):** If validated, design 5kW MHD system for field test

**Potential Efficiency:** IF proven: Direct conversion could reach 90%+!  
**Risk:** 80% chance of failure (requires breakthrough in MHD scaling)  
**Expected Value:** 0.2 × 90% + 0.8 × 73% = **76.4% (weighted average)**

**DECISION:** Pursue MHD as long-term R&D (5-10 year horizon). High risk, high reward.
